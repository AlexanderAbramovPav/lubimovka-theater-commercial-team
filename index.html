From School

A play about the demonic charm of a vocational school student and her fatal role in life a philosophy teacher.

May 12, 20:00

Semyon Serzin did a reading of this play as part of the main program Lyubimovka-2017, the Brewhouse provided a grant for the production, and the Moscow Drama Theater named after A.S. Pushkin accepted the play into the repertoire.

1 h. 15 min. | 18+

Playwright

Robert Askins

Translation

Oksana Alyoshina

Text adaptation

Valery Pecheykin

Director

Yuri Muravitsky

Actors

Fedor Kokorev, Kolya Noekeln, Ksenia Chekina, Alexander Pronkin, Vladimir Morozov, Svetlana Marshankina

The play "The Hand of the Lord" (Hand to God) — winner of the American Theater Award OBIE Awards (Off-Broadway Theater Awards), nominee of the annual TONY Award (Theatre Of New York), the theatrical equivalent of the Oscar, and a nominee for the British Laurence Olivier Award as "Best New Comedies". It has been staged in Broadway theaters and in London's West End.

This performance is the result of the collaboration of the Lyubimovka Drama Festival, which launched a project to produce its own performances, Lyubimovka.Production", Theater 18+ and Театра.doc . The performers are graduates of the Yuri Muravitsky Acting Laboratory of the Moscow School of New Cinema.

The director of the play Yuri Muravitsky is a laureate of the National Theater Golden Mask Awards, winner of the Herald Angel Award and Total Theatre Award in the Innovation nomination, Experimentation & Playing with Form at the Edinburgh Festival Fringe: "In this play, I am touched by her courage and freedom. And the fact that it is written funny. I'm interested in comedies right now. In January I released "Le Tartuffe. Comedy" at the Taganka Theater is also a comedy and also in a certain sense anticlerical. I have a persistent the feeling that everything that is happening now in our country and in the world, we can only oppose laughter. Sometimes bitter laughter, sometimes angry. There's so much hate in the air right now that it's good for us all to laugh more. And over myself as well. "The Hand of the Lord" is a black comedy. Among other things, it shows what it leads to the imposition and forcible imposition of religion."

The play was successfully presented in Russia in the format of a reading in 2016 . LARK Labs+Lyubimovka on the stage of the Center. Meyerhold.
